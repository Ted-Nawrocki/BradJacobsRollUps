The Career Arc

Brad Jacobs was born August 3, 1956, in Providence, Rhode Island. He attended Northfield Mount Hermon School and later studied at Bennington College and Brown University. His career began not in finance or operations, but in commodity trading. In 1979, at the age of 22, he co-founded Amerex Oil Associates, an oil brokerage firm that he and his team built to $4.7 billion in gross contract volume before selling the business in 1983.

After a period of independent oil trading in London, Jacobs returned to the United States in 1989 and applied a strategy that would define the next three decades: identify a large, fragmented industry, enter via acquisition, and consolidate aggressively. That first U.S. application was United Waste Systems, Inc.

The Consolidation Formula

United Waste targeted rural solid waste collection companies. The economics were compelling: small operators serving overlapping routes carried duplicative fixed costs, thin technology, and no access to institutional capital. Jacobs bought these operators sequentially, layered shared services across the combined platform, and drove margin expansion through route density. He took United Waste public in 1992. By the time he sold the company to USA Waste Services in August 1997 for $2.5 billion, the stock had outperformed the S&P 500 by 5.6 times from IPO to sale.

The same month he closed that sale, Jacobs co-founded United Rentals. He applied an identical model to the equipment rental industry, building the company into the world's largest equipment rental operator in thirteen months through a combination of acquisitions and organic branch openings. He took United Rentals public on the New York Stock Exchange in December 1997. Today, United Rentals is a Fortune 500 company and has been one of the six best-performing stocks of the past decade in that index.

XPO, GXO, and the Logistics Platform

In 2011, Jacobs invested approximately $150 million in Express-1 Expedited Solutions, a small freight brokerage company with revenues of $175 million, and became its chairman and CEO. He renamed it XPO Logistics and announced an intention to grow revenue to $5 billion within five years primarily through M&A. He exceeded that target substantially, reaching $15 billion in revenue in just over four years through a series of large acquisitions including the $3.5 billion purchase of Con-way Inc. and the $3.6 billion acquisition of Norbert Dentressangle.

Jacobs subsequently led two major spin-offs from XPO: GXO Logistics in August 2021 and RXO in November 2022, creating two independent publicly traded companies in roughly fifteen months. GXO is now the world's largest pure-play contract logistics provider. XPO's stock became a 50-bagger for initial investors, and the company ranked as the seventh-best-performing Fortune 500 stock of the past decade, according to Bloomberg market data.

QXO and Building Products

In December 2023, Jacobs publicly announced plans to build a dominant platform in building products distribution. In June 2024, QXO was formed through the acquisition of SilverSun Technologies, Inc., a shell company renamed QXO, Inc. and traded on the Nasdaq before transferring to the NYSE. Jacobs raised approximately $5 billion in private placement financing by mid-2024, including a $900 million commitment from his own vehicle, Jacobs Private Equity, and $100 million from Sequoia Heritage and other co-investors, alongside a $3.5 billion private placement closed in July 2024.

The stated thesis: the building products distribution industry generates approximately $800 billion in annual North American and European revenues, is served by roughly 20,000 operators, and has historically resisted technology adoption. Jacobs targets $50 billion in annual revenues within a decade through acquisitions and organic growth, representing approximately 6% of the total addressable market.
