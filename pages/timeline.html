GMS Inc. (NYSE: GMS)
Gypsum Management and Supply — Interior construction products distributor
~$95/share
QXO Offer Price

QXO made a bid for GMS Inc. at approximately $95 per share. GMS is a distributor of gypsum wallboard, ceilings, and other interior construction products. The bid represented QXO's initial public acquisition attempt following capital formation. Home Depot countered through its subsidiary SRS Distribution, ultimately acquiring GMS in September 2024 for approximately $5.5 billion (approximately $110 per share). Jacobs declined to increase QXO's offer, stating that the company was unwilling to overpay. The decision to walk away rather than enter a bidding war is consistent with Jacobs' stated capital discipline.

Beacon Roofing Supply, Inc.
Now QXO Building Products, Inc. — Largest publicly traded U.S. roofing & waterproofing distributor at acquisition
$11B
Total Enterprise Value

The Beacon acquisition was QXO's anchor transaction and the foundation of its building products platform. Beacon was the largest publicly traded distributor of roofing, waterproofing, and complementary building products in the United States at the time of acquisition, with approximately 600 branches across the U.S. and Canada and 2024 revenues of approximately $9.8 billion.

QXO initially commenced a tender offer on January 27, 2025 at $124.25 per share. On March 20, 2025, QXO entered into a formal Agreement and Plan of Merger, and on March 31, 2025, the tender offer was amended to increase the offer price to $124.35 per share. The Merger Agreement was by and among QXO, Beacon, and Queen MergerCo, Inc., a Delaware corporation and wholly owned subsidiary of QXO formed as the merger vehicle. The acquisition closed April 29, 2025. Beacon was renamed QXO Building Products, Inc. following the merger.

To finance the acquisition, QXO completed a registered public offering of $2 billion of common stock, with Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, and Wells Fargo as joint lead bookrunning managers (Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton & Garrison LLP as legal counsel).

Kodiak Building Partners
National distributor of lumber, trusses, windows, doors, construction supplies — 110 locations across 26 states
$2.25B
Total Consideration

Kodiak Building Partners was acquired from Court Square Capital Partners, a New York-based private equity firm. Founded in 2011, Kodiak had grown to 110 locations across 26 states under Court Square's eight-year ownership, generating approximately $2.4 billion in revenue in 2025. The business operates under locally branded names including American Builders Supply (Florida), Simonson Lumber (Minnesota), Zarsky Lumber (Texas), and Shepley Wood Products (Massachusetts), among others.

The strategic rationale centered on product category expansion: 16 of Kodiak's top 20 vendors are also Beacon suppliers, providing immediate procurement overlap and cross-selling opportunity. Kodiak's structural products (lumber, trusses, windows, doors) complement Beacon's exterior products (roofing, waterproofing, siding), and together the combined platform can supply large homebuilders across the full exterior project lifecycle. Approximately 40% of Kodiak's 2025 revenues derived from Florida and Texas, markets with housing growth rates consistently above national averages.

The transaction closed April 1, 2026. Steve Swinney, Kodiak's co-founder and CEO, now leads QXO's new lumber and building materials (LBM) division. QXO stated at announcement that the transaction was expected to be highly accretive to 2026 earnings.

TopBuild Corp. (NYSE: BLD)
Leading installer and distributor of insulation and related building products — 450+ locations, U.S. and Canada
$17B
Total Enterprise Value

The TopBuild acquisition is QXO's largest transaction to date and the most strategically significant in terms of expanding the platform's addressable market and product coverage. TopBuild is the leading installer and distributor of insulation and related products in the United States, serving residential, commercial, and industrial end markets with products including fiberglass and mineral wool insulation, gutters, fireproofing materials, and mechanical insulation. The company operates more than 450 locations across the U.S. and Canada and generated approximately $6.2 billion in net sales in 2025.

Under the terms of the Agreement, TopBuild stockholders may elect to receive either $505 per share in cash or 20.2 shares of QXO common stock per TopBuild share. The election is subject to proration: the total transaction consideration is structured as approximately 45% cash and 55% QXO stock in aggregate. The $505 per share price represents a premium of 19.8% to TopBuild's 60-day volume-weighted average price and a premium of 23.1% to TopBuild's closing price on April 17, 2026 (the trading day prior to announcement).

QXO states that upon completion, the combined company will be the second-largest publicly traded building products distributor in North America, with more than $18 billion in combined revenue, more than $2 billion in combined adjusted EBITDA, approximately 28,000 employees, and 1,150 locations. Both companies' boards of directors unanimously approved the transaction. Closing is subject to TopBuild shareholder approval, regulatory clearance, and other customary conditions. A Form S-4 registration statement is expected to be filed with the SEC.
