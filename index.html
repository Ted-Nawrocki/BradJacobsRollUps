The Thesis

Same Playbook.
Different Industry.

Brad Jacobs has executed the same fundamental strategy across four decades and four industries: identify a large, fragmented market with thin technology penetration, raise institutional capital at scale before acquiring a single company, then execute rapid acquisitions to build a dominant national platform.

The pattern is deliberate and replicable. United Waste Systems consolidated rural solid waste collection. United Rentals consolidated equipment rental dealerships. XPO Logistics consolidated freight brokerage and contract logistics. Each time, Jacobs entered a market where the largest competitor held a low single-digit market share, deployed M&A to build scale advantages unavailable to subscale operators, and created shareholder value through the arithmetic of multiple expansion and operational leverage.

QXO, launched in June 2024, targets building products distribution in North America. The industry generates approximately $800 billion in annual revenue, is served by roughly 20,000 independent distributors across the U.S. and Europe, and has historically resisted consolidation. That fragmentation is not a flaw in Jacobs' thesis. It is the thesis.

Within eleven months of Jacobs taking operational control, QXO had committed over $13 billion to acquisitions. Within twenty-three months, announced deals exceeded $30 billion in aggregate consideration. This site documents the mechanics, the deal terms, and the analytical framework behind that trajectory.
